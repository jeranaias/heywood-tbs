# Prompt 17: Peer Evaluation Structuring

**Use:** Help SPCs structure peer evaluation feedback into actionable development insights
**Platform:** GenAI.mil
**Complexity:** Beginner
**Source:** Custom for TBS (no direct EDD analog)

---

## Prompt

```
I am a Staff Platoon Commander (SPC) at The Basic School. I've completed peer evaluations for my platoon and need to synthesize the results into usable counseling inputs.

EVALUATION POINT: [Week 12 Midpoint / Week 22 Final]
PLATOON SIZE: [NUMBER_OF_STUDENTS]

PEER EVALUATION DATA (anonymized — use billet positions, not names):
[FOR EACH STUDENT, PROVIDE:
- Billet position (e.g., "1st Squad Leader," "Plt Sgt," "3rd Fire Team Leader")
- Peer ranking: [TOP / UPPER MIDDLE / LOWER MIDDLE / BOTTOM THIRD]
- Common peer feedback themes: [2-3 word descriptions — e.g., "strong under pressure, poor communicator, reliable, disorganized, good tactical sense, struggles with time management"]
]

Help me analyze these peer evaluations:

1. **Consensus Check** — Where does peer feedback ALIGN with my SPC assessment? Where does it DISAGREE? (Disagreements are the most important — they reveal blind spots in my evaluation or the students' self-awareness.)

2. **Theme Analysis** — What leadership themes emerge across the platoon?
   - Most commonly cited strengths across all students
   - Most commonly cited weaknesses across all students
   - Any students who are rated very differently by peers than by me (positive or negative)

3. **Counseling Integration** — For each student:
   - One sentence summarizing peer perception
   - Whether this changes, reinforces, or contradicts my assessment
   - Suggested talking point for the counseling session (e.g., "Your peers see you as reliable but not assertive — does that match your self-assessment?")

4. **Calibration Notes** — Am I seeing any patterns that suggest:
   - Peer evaluations are just a popularity contest (top-ranked are the most social, not the most competent)?
   - Students are gaming the system (always ranking friends high)?
   - Genuine performance signal that I should weight in my final evaluation?

Format the output as a reference document I can have open during counseling sessions. NOT for student distribution.
```

---

## Notes
- NEVER use student names in the prompt — use billet positions only
- Peer evaluations are 10% of the leadership grade — they inform but don't determine the SPC's assessment
- The "Consensus Check" is the highest-value section — disagreements between peer and SPC assessment are where development conversations happen
- This output is for SPC use ONLY, never shared with students
